As a result, we can make certain assumptions when thinking about people falling through the sky. Firstly, they fall directly towards the surface in more or less a straight line. Secondly, the time it takes to fall a certain distance is the same regardless of how heavy a person is. Physicists like to write mathematical relations to describe these observations.
